mountains

Home is where the alpine meets the high desert. I am powered by sun beams, and spend as much time as possible in the mountains. I love to climb, though I began my outdoor journey with hiking and backpacking long distances, and so I actually don't mind long approaches.

I have historically disliked running because I started this life as a swimmer and found sweating unsettling and impact unnatural, but then I had the startling realisation that trail running is like hiking, but really fast. I run trails with my dog, who makes amusing company.

In the summer, I hike my foldable boat out to paddle my favourite alpine lakes.

In my quest to develop love for winter, I am going skiing this season! (if I don't get too distracted by bouldering).

values

I value enthusiasm for the moment yet appropriate detachment from things and outcomes, and cultivate a flexible, resilient coexistence with the world: I am comfortable in my skin but also ready to leave on a long trip or to restart my life in less than 24 hours notice. When I'm not on a big ramble, I see small opportunities for tiny adventures hidden in the folds of daily life, the magical ingredient being a spare moment for serendipity. I long to be rich in time. Then I would donate time to this time-improverished nation.

During the pandemic shelter-in-place, I started a hearty yoga practice, initially in a desperate attempt to prevent my soul from collapsing, but now I'm enjoying the benefits of harnessing my butt to accomplish gravity-defying feats, and knowing how to manually unclench my chest when the world is being crushing (because if you aren't a quilt of coping mechanisms masquerading as wholesome hobbies are you even a modern person?)

work

I like to work on developer tools empowering engineers.

Why work? I have low comfort needs and no dependents, it's simple for me to live nomadically for long stretches of time, doing my hobbies and picking up new tricks, meeting people and making open source without sponsors. Having the slow world of dirtbags, hostels, hitched rides, intentional communities and mountains to compare to, here's what's appealing about the software industry: it's filled with highly motivated, smart, creative, and kind people swirling with ideas, opinions, and urgency. This is a energy that I like to marinate myself with to become overflowing with the desire to do big things and participate in ambitious projects that don't exist in the underfunded wild. I learn a lot about technology, communication, project management, and navigating human organisation formal and invisible. On my best days I tell myself I am get paid to be around cool people. (On my hardest days, the American work ethic crushes me and clashes with everything else that makes me feel alive–freedom, time, community). Balance is impossible if you seek experiences and like to create and expect a lot from yourself, and harbor a irreverence towards the implied ideal life trajectory, so I go for what I think I want, and reevaluate until it isn't true anymore.
